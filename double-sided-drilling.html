DIFFERENTIATION

Two Engineering Innovations That Redefine Hole Drilling

Both-side simultaneous drilling and a horizontal architecture lift quality, machinability, and throughput at the same time.

01

Both-Side Simultaneous Drilling

Eliminates the workpiece-flip step — the largest source of concentricity error in conventional drilling.

BEFORE

Single-Direction Type

  • Drill one face, then flip and re-fixture for a 2-step process
  • Pitch error and concentricity defects introduced during the flip
  • Total cycle time grows; operator intervention raises labor cost
  • Alignment error reduces yield
→
AFTER

Both-Side Type

  • Two spindles drill simultaneously from both faces — no flip required
  • Concentricity held within 20㎛ at all times
  • Hole quality preserved even when machine pitch error occurs
  • Longer unattended runs — higher yield, more output
02

Horizontal Machining Structure

A horizontal hole axis lets chips evacuate naturally — the key enabler for true deep-hole drilling.

BEFORE

Vertical Type

  • Gravity packs swarf inside the hole
  • Reduced coolant flow — risk of drill breakage
  • Hard ceiling on deep-hole capability
→
AFTER

Horizontal Type

  • Chips evacuate horizontally by gravity — drill protected
  • 1.5–2× throughput at identical cutting conditions
  • 2–3× throughput with optimized recipe
